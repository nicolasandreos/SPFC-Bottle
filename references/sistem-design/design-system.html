06 · Guidelines

Principles for using the system.

Practical rules that keep the visual language intact across products, teams and time.

Color

✓
Do

Use red as a single, deliberate accent — for primary actions, focus, and emotional emphasis.

×
Don't

Don't paint surfaces in red. Reserve it for moments that matter; over-use dilutes the system.

✓
Do

Pair red with deep ink and generous white to maintain a premium, editorial contrast.

×
Don't

Don't introduce new accent colors. The system is intentionally monochromatic with one warm signal.

Typography

✓
Do

Use Fraunces italic to highlight a single emotional word inside a serif heading.

×
Don't

Don't mix serif and sans inside the same heading. Let the families do their job.

✓
Do

Cap line length around 60 characters for body, 24 for display headings.

Motion

✓
Do

Lead with --ease-out for entrances. It's the system's voice.

×
Don't

Don't animate everything. Motion should clarify hierarchy, not compete with content.

✓
Do

Honor prefers-reduced-motion. Replace transforms with simple fades.

×
Don't

Don't chain more than 3 sequenced reveals on the same screen. The eye gets tired.

Components & Spacing

✓
Do

Compose layouts using only the spacing scale. Custom values break the rhythm.

✓
Do

Always provide hover, focus-visible, active, disabled and loading states for interactive components.

×
Don't

Don't fork components per page. Extend variants in the system itself.
